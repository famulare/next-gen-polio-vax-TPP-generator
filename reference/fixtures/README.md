# Reference fixtures

The locked source repositories and commits are recorded in
`src/data/provenance.json`. The committed fixtures are source-derived direct
ports and calibration artifacts:

- `india-r-susceptibility-v1.json` records the India R WPV/Sabin per-bin
  susceptibility kernel over the full declared hard-bound grid. Cases in the
  source-defined low-dose branch are diagnostics that intentionally
  discriminate the browser's exact contracted equation;
- `india-r-vaccine-take-v1.json` checks one-dose take/no-take conditioning and
  boosting across the declared lower/default/higher product grid;
- `india-r-comparators-v1.json` checks Sabin boost-transition matrices across
  the hypothetical boost range, fixed Sabin-2/IPV one-, three-, and four-dose
  schedule composition, and the fixed catalog's history-dependent IPV
  mucosal/serum semantics;
- `india-r-schedule-v1.json` checks one-, three-, and four-dose composition
  plus each selected schedule/assessment waning interval across
  lower/default/higher hypothetical products;
- `india-r-shedding-v1.json` records source shedding survival and joint
  intensity across the declared bin/day/age grid as diagnostics. Its rounded
  duration/titer constants and age-amplitude equation deliberately do **not**
  replace the browser's exact Cessation constants and seven-month
  plateau/offset;
- `cessation-matlab-motif-v1.json` is generated by executing the pinned
  Cessation Matlab motif through Octave. It records full daily fixed-titer
  primary, secondary, and tertiary incidence plus the low, moderate, Matlab
  hybrid, and high named-anchor cases.
- `cessation-calibration-prevalence-v1.json` records source-executed,
  infection-conditioned day-1--45 prevalence targets and their explicit
  browser state mappings; and
- `calibration-report-v1.json` is the deterministic Section 15.2 report. It
  fits the approved Matlab index mean, jointly fits the constrained India
  contact mean and `T_ih`, and fits Matlab `T_ih`; it verifies every
  target-role profile against the locked `0.1 log10` RMSE gate and records the
  declared India coarse/refinement grids, fitted values, and diagnostics.

The Cessation fixture also supports the test-only fixed-titer compatibility
port. It does not replace the browser's distribution-native transmission path.
`manifest-v1.json` records SHA-256 hashes, source branches,
tracked/untracked state, declared source files, actual files read, generator
command, schema version, and the remaining required coverage.

`scripts/generate-reference-fixtures.R` is an executable, fail-closed
generator for these artifacts. It reads provenance, verifies exact locked
commits, rejects tracked source changes, validates every declared source file,
and invokes Octave for the Cessation Matlab motif. `test:fixtures` exercises
the missing/dirty/provenance failures against temporary Git repositories;
`check:reference-fixtures` verifies the committed artifacts and manifest.
After regenerating source fixtures, run `npm run generate:calibration-report`;
`check:calibration` verifies that the report and its manifest hash are current.

Under the 2026-07-17 Design Contract amendment, the manifest's direct-port
grid coverage plus the passing Section 15.2 prevalence calibration satisfy the
Section 15.1 hybrid parity gate for this iteration. Each individual source
artifact retains `releaseGateSatisfied: false`: a single kernel or scalar-motif
fixture is never an application-release decision. The Cessation fixed-titer
`R_loc` anchors and Section 15.2 `sourceRLocContextOnly` values remain
context/calibration records, not fixed Sabin/IPV product-schedule transmission
parity. The [source audit](../../docs/release-blocker-source-audit.md) records
why an original-source product-schedule transmission fixture does not exist and
is not a parity requirement for this prototype.

The [backend fixture-input authority audit](../../docs/backend-source-authority-audit.md)
classifies every injected generator constant as source, contract/grid input,
or explicit diagnostic where those authorities differ.
