> **Historical review record.** This document assesses commit `46e27fb` and
> its then-locked contract. Its release-blocker conclusions are superseded by
> the 2026-07-17 Design Contract 1.5 amendment, expanded direct-port fixture
> grids, passing Section 15.2 report, and point-rule-only scope. The findings
> remain useful provenance; current requirements are in `DESIGN_CONTRACT.md`
> and `IMPLEMENTATION_LOG.md`.

**Review status:** implementation is a useful internally consistent prototype,
but is **not release-eligible under the locked v1 contract**.

**Reviewed artifact:** commit `46e27fb` (`main`), including the committed
`dist/index.html`.

**Scope:** implementation adherence to `DESIGN_CONTRACT.md`, model mechanics,
schema/audit boundaries, source-reference readiness, and release checks. This
review deliberately does **not** re-evaluate biological plausibility. A targeted

## Executive conclusion

The current implementation has several positive mechanical properties: it is
deterministic, preserves probability mass in its tested operations, uses the
declared 51 x 51 product grid and 81 x 40 setting grid, keeps contact frequency
outside the dose-response function, and passes its local TypeScript, model,
build, and self-contained-artifact checks.

Those checks are not enough to support a source-validated TPP conclusion. The
contract makes R/Matlab parity and a reviewed uncertainty ensemble release
gates. At the reviewed baseline there were no reference fixtures, no
calibration results, and the fixture-generator entry point failed to parse.
The current worktree supplies an explicitly partial R/Matlab fixture suite and
a passing Section 15.2 prevalence-calibration report, but not a full source
suite or reviewed uncertainty ensemble. In addition, several serializable scenario
states are accepted even though they change product or setting meaning. These
are not presentation defects: they can cause a result to be calculated under a
different model than its label implies.

Until full Section 15.1 parity and a reviewed uncertainty ensemble are
complete, numerical output should be called **prototype model output, pending
source parity and reviewed uncertainty**. It must not be used to set a product
target or support a claim that a candidate meets the v1 rule.

## Verified findings

### P0 — Source parity and calibration are unavailable at the reviewed baseline

**Evidence**

- `reference/fixtures/` contained only a README; no R/Matlab parity fixtures
  were bundled.
- `scripts/generate-reference-fixtures.R` contained an invalid R escape in the
  tracked-status regex (`"^\?\?"`), so it exited before it could perform its
  stated source-state check.
- Even after that syntax error was repaired, the original script only checked
  checkout status and printed a message; it did not generate, version, or
  compare any reference fixture.
- The uncertainty generator intentionally fails because no reviewed joint
  ensemble exists. That fail-closed behavior is appropriate, but it leaves the
  upper-95 rule unavailable.

**Why this matters**

The locked contract requires parity for the biological kernels, schedule
composition, primary-secondary-tertiary motif, and published anchors. It also
requires a distribution-native calibration gate before accepting the modernized
`R_loc`. Internal invariants cannot establish that the reduction retains the
reference model's numerical meaning or threshold classifications.

**Required fix**

Implement the fixture and calibration workflow in Phases 2-3. Do not loosen a
tolerance or introduce an undocumented multiplier to make a fixture pass.

### P1 — Product identity is mutable in imported scenario state

**Evidence**

`validateVaccine()` validates `id` and `live` independently. A serialized
scenario labelled `IPV` can therefore set `live: true`, a nonzero dose, and
live-vaccine take/boost parameters. `applyDose()` branches on that mutable
boolean, not the product id. A direct probe verified that this malformed but
accepted `IPV` scenario produces the live-product maximum `R_loc` rather than
the canonical all-IPV result.

**Why this matters**

This violates the fixed comparator catalog and means that a shareable URL can
display an IPV label while calculating an OPV-like schedule. It is a semantic
model error, not merely weak input hygiene.

**Required fix**

Treat product behavior as a catalog-derived discriminated union:

1. derive `live`, label, and fixed comparator parameters from `ProductId`;
2. reject any imported Sabin 2 or IPV values that differ from their catalog
   definitions;
3. allow only the declared hypothetical-product fields to vary, while enforcing
   fixed `gamma` and `sigma0`;
4. add regression tests for malformed IPV, malformed Sabin 2, and mismatched
   label/product state.

### P1 — Selected custom setting is not evaluated as a selected setting

**Evidence**

The model's main result is always calculated at `envelopeCorner(scenario)`.
Named anchors are calculated separately, but the custom `scenario.setting`
record is not used in `computePointMetrics()`. A direct probe with custom zero
contacts/exposure produced the same maximum `R_loc` (`28.93754322604756`),
frontier, and setting surface as the default scenario.

**Why this matters**

The scenario carries one setting object while the model reports another. This
breaks reproducibility and can misstate what a reported setting-specific value
means.

**Required fix**

Make the two calculations explicit and separately named:

- `rLocSelectedSetting = rLocForSetting(state, scenario.setting, ...)` for the
  selected named/custom probe; and
- `rLocEnvelopeMax` for the v1 decision rule.

The summary, export, and tests must preserve both values and their different
interpretations. A regression test must show that zero custom exposure or
contacts yields selected-setting `R_loc = 0` while leaving the independently
declared envelope maximum unchanged.

### P1 — Envelope schema permits states the model cannot represent

**Evidence**

- `EnvelopeV1.linkedExposure` is accepted, but setting it to `false` does not
  change the setting surface or envelope calculation.
- The schema accepts `TMin = 0`; subsequent surface evaluation reaches an
  invalid dose-response state rather than rejecting the scenario at validation
  time.
- One shared `TMin/TMax` pair cannot represent independent `T_ih` and `T_hs`
  bounds even though the contract says an advanced path may unlink them.

**Why this matters**

The contract requires invalid scientific state to fail closed with an
actionable validation error. It also requires envelope maxima to correspond to
the declared axes. A no-op schema field invites a reader to believe an
unmodelled distinction was evaluated.

**Required fix**

Either implement the contracted unlinked envelope with `TihMin/TihMax` and
`ThsMin/ThsMax`, or remove `linkedExposure` through a contract amendment. In
either case, validate strictly before calculation: positive finite exposure
bounds, ordered bounds, and only supported unit/basis combinations.

### P1 — Frontier mutation breaks fixed-comparator semantics

**Evidence**

`buildFrontier()` replaces `takeContext` and `mu0` for every selected product.
For Sabin 2 this turns a fixed comparator into a grid of altered Sabin-like
products; for IPV the grid is degenerate because it remains non-live.

**Why this matters**

The product-design frontier is defined for the hypothetical OPV-like pathway.
Fixed comparators should be evaluated at their catalog points, not silently
turned into a different product family.

**Required fix**

Build the 51 x 51 frontier only from a hypothetical-product template using the
current schedule and fixed-by-control parameters. Evaluate Sabin 2 and IPV as
separate fixed comparator points and carry their exact product identities in
the model output/export schema.

### P2 — Verification, provenance, and release records are incomplete

**Evidence**

- The repository has ten internal model tests but no parity test directory or
  source-derived fixtures.
- Required invariant families are only partially covered; for example, the
  current test suite does not establish the full-domain shedding monotonicity,
  all requested schema failures, cache invalidation, or anchor reproduction.
- `provenance.json` records source commits and source paths but not the fixture
  generator command, branch, dirty/untracked state, fixture schema version, or
  generated-file hashes required by the contract.
- The current SVG export omits the required setting surface, and the release
  footer has a version but no commit identity. These are auditability gaps,
  not scientific-model changes.

**Required fix**

Expand the test and release work in Phase 4. Every displayed scientific result
must be reproducible from a canonical scenario, manifest content, direct grid
outputs, and complete figure exports.

## Targeted source-port observations

The limited code-to-code review did **not** identify an obvious divergence in
the inspected pieces:

- TypeScript susceptibility uses the same beta-Poisson expression, GH5
  quadrature strategy, and naive-versus-waned bin-0 distinction as the pinned
  India implementation.
- Live-dose propagation conditions take and no-take mass separately before
  boosting, consistent with the reference tilting pattern.
- Shedder-side bin 0 is treated as a Dirac state, matching the India source's
  current explicit "Option 4" convention.

This is a narrow observation, not numerical certification. In particular, the
India susceptibility implementation uses a documented low-dose linear regime;
the browser uses the exact expression everywhere. Fixtures must determine
whether that intentional difference remains within the locked tolerance over
the full relevant domain.

## Remediation plan

### Phase 0 — Release posture and test harness recovery

**Objective:** prevent an incomplete model from being interpreted as a
source-validated decision tool, and restore the fixture entry point.

**Status:** complete. The current generator produces a deliberately partial
artifact only after strict provenance validation; its manifest states that the
Section 15 gates are unsatisfied.

1. Mark the current artifact and release notes as prototype/pending parity and
   calibration. Preserve the existing correct fail-closed behavior for the
   upper-95 rule.
2. Fix the R regex syntax error in `generate-reference-fixtures.R`.
3. Add a CI test that executes the generator in a controlled source-checkout
   fixture and verifies that it either emits versioned data or fails with the
   specific expected provenance/cleanliness error.

**Exit criteria:** the generator runs; no app or release note claims parity,
calibration, or upper-95 support without the required artifacts.

### Phase 1 — Make the scenario schema authoritative

**Objective:** eliminate states whose label and calculation disagree.

1. Replace mutable product behavior with a catalog-backed product schema.
2. Canonicalize/validate named anchors; a named anchor id must resolve to its
   manifest values, while custom is the only arbitrary setting record.
3. Split selected-setting evaluation from envelope evaluation in the output
   type, serialization, exports, and model API.
4. Implement independent link bounds or remove the unsupported unlinked flag
   through a formal contract amendment.
5. Reject zero/nonfinite/out-of-domain exposure bounds before model execution.

**Required tests:** malformed fixed comparators; custom-zero setting;
named-anchor mismatch; linked/unlinked behavior; zero/min/max/nonfinite/unit
envelope states; URL decode failure with an actionable error.

**Exit criteria:** every serializable scenario has one unambiguous scientific
meaning, and every visible/exported setting result is calculated from the
setting it names.

### Phase 2 — Generate reference fixtures from pinned sources

**Objective:** create auditable numerical targets rather than copied constants.

**Status:** in progress. India R susceptibility, one-dose vaccine-conditioning,
and shedding fixtures, plus their hash/provenance checks, are complete. They
are intentionally insufficient for this phase's exit criteria.

1. Implement source extractors for the pinned India R code and cessation Matlab
   motif at the commits recorded in `src/data/provenance.json`.
2. Generate fixtures for all Section 15.1 kernel families, including fixed
   titer primary-secondary-tertiary incidence and low/moderate/high `R_loc`.
3. Write a versioned fixture manifest recording: repository commits and
   branches, tracked dirty flag, untracked paths, source files read, generator
   command, fixture schema version, grid/domain, and hashes.
4. Commit fixtures, generator code, and a concise review of their first diff.

**Exit criteria:** fixtures generate reproducibly from clean locked sources and
direct-port tests pass at the locked `1e-8` / `1e-5` tolerances.

### Phase 3 — Pass the faithful-modernization calibration gate

**Objective:** establish that distribution-native transmission preserves the
reference control conclusion, not merely its implementation shape.

1. Add a degenerate-distribution compatibility mode/test for reference
   fixed-titer bins.
2. Evaluate modern central `R_loc` at every required anchor/calibration case.
3. Require agreement within `0.1 log10`, no `R_loc = 1` crossing caused by the
   modernization, and no reversal of low/moderate/high control conclusions.
4. If this fails, stop and produce the contract-required versioned refit
   artifact. Do not repair it with an undocumented multiplier.

**Exit criteria:** a checked-in calibration report and tests satisfy both
levels of Section 15.2.

### Phase 4 — Complete invariant, export, and release assurance

**Objective:** make model behavior and each displayed result auditable.

1. Add exhaustive or property-based invariant tests over the declared domain:
   probability/mass bounds, susceptibility and shedding monotonicity, all
   setting axes, take/boost monotonicity, direct-grid maxima, frontier ties,
   and cache invalidation.
2. Add exact URL/export round-trip tests, including manifest identity.
3. Export all figures, including the setting surface, and preserve the full
   evaluated grid fields (`Tih`, `Ths`, `dIh`, `dHs`, `Ns`) in CSV.
4. Add app commit identity, provenance links, artifact hash recording, and the
   GitHub Pages path-prefix test required by the contract.
5. Add timed checks for the contract performance budgets. The review measured a
   fresh frontier at about 0.64 seconds and the full default output at about
   2.08 seconds on this machine; these are observations, not portable
   performance certification.

**Exit criteria:** `npm run verify` covers every contract gate, a clean CI
rebuild is byte-identical, and the release record identifies code, manifests,
fixtures, and artifact hash.

### Phase 5 — Independent UI/UX and interpretation review

**Objective:** assess the widget as a decision aid for a non-modeler only
after the model has one unambiguous, source-validated state boundary.

**Required review scope**

1. Interact with the built artifact through an approved local browser surface
   and document each tested product, anchor, custom setting, schedule, export,
   keyboard path, narrow viewport, invalid URL, and unavailable upper-95 path.
2. Assess whether a non-modeler can accurately state:
   - the decision rule and its envelope;
   - the difference between a selected-setting probe and the global-envelope
     pass/fail result;
   - what acquisition blocking and breakthrough infectious shedding mean;
   - why the scalar shedding index is diagnostic rather than a pass axis;
   - what is modelled, assumed, source-validated, unavailable, and out of
     scope.
3. Assess whether fixed comparators and the hypothetical product are visibly
   distinct and whether the maps, contours, colors, tooltips, controls, and
   exports support the same interpretation.

**Design reference and desired direction**

Use
`/Users/famulare/git/famulare/IDM-archival-recovery/private/exhibits/the-work-between-messages.html`
as a reference for information architecture and textual support, **not** as a
visual template to copy. Its useful qualities to adapt are:

- an immediate statement of the question and boundary;
- deliberate chapter-level progression rather than a dense control wall;
- short explanatory text beside every consequential visualization;
- explicit labels distinguishing evidence/derived result/assumption/gap; and
- a coherent visual hierarchy that makes the reader's next question obvious.

For this tool, the equivalent progression should be **What product is enough?
Why does it work or fail? Where does the envelope bind? What has and has not
been validated?** The page should retain Luna's apparent model complexity while
making the meaning of each output legible without prior modeling expertise.

**Exit criteria:** an independent reviewer can complete the required scenarios
without misreading the decision rule or uncertainty status; findings are
converted into explicit accessibility, content, and interaction acceptance
tests before implementation.

## Decision checkpoints

1. **After Phase 1:** approve the canonical scenario semantics before changing
   any scientific default.
2. **After Phase 2:** inspect fixture provenance and parity diffs.
3. **After Phase 3:** decide whether the calibration gate permits v1 release or
   requires a documented reduced-layer refit.
4. **After Phase 5:** approve the interaction/content contract before visual
   redesign; do not let visual polish mask a still-provisional model result.

No scientific defaults, threshold tolerances, or success criteria should change
without a design-contract amendment and explicit review.
